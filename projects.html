Our Projects

Making measurable impact through evidence-based interventions

CHERD Africa has successfully implemented diverse health research and development projects across multiple domains, partnering with international organizations to improve health outcomes for African communities.

Featured Projects

Global Fund

Community, Rights and Gender Assessment of Tuberculosis in Ghana

Comprehensive assessment of TB programs in Ghana, focusing on community engagement, human rights aspects, and gender-specific challenges. This project aims to improve treatment outcomes, reduce stigma, and ensure equitable access to TB services.

Key Objectives:
  • Assess community engagement in TB programs
  • Evaluate rights-based approaches to TB care
  • Analyze gender disparities in TB diagnosis and treatment
  • Develop recommendations for policy improvement
GIZ

Evaluation of Evidence-Based Intervention - Akoma Pa

Rigorous evaluation of the Akoma Pa innovation, an evidence-based intervention designed to improve healthcare delivery in underserved communities. The study assesses effectiveness, scalability, and sustainability of the intervention model.

Key Objectives:
  • Measure intervention effectiveness and impact
  • Assess implementation fidelity and quality
  • Evaluate scalability and sustainability potential
  • Document best practices and lessons learned
IDRC

Reducing Child Mortality Through Gender Disparity Analysis

Research project analyzing gender disparities in child health outcomes and developing targeted interventions to reduce child mortality rates. The study examines social, cultural, and healthcare system factors contributing to differential outcomes.

Key Objectives:
  • Analyze gender disparities in child mortality
  • Identify contributing social and cultural factors
  • Develop gender-sensitive interventions
  • Build evidence base for policy advocacy
WAPCAS

Persons Living with HIV Stigma Index Study

Comprehensive study measuring stigma and discrimination experienced by persons living with HIV. The research provides critical data to inform anti-stigma programs and improve quality of life for people living with HIV.

Key Objectives:
  • Measure levels of HIV-related stigma and discrimination
  • Assess impact on healthcare access and quality of life
  • Identify drivers of stigma in communities
  • Inform development of anti-stigma interventions
Respiratory Health

Respiratory Health Improvement Initiative

Implementation of health improvement interventions in rural and peri-urban settlements near Kumasi, focusing on pediatric respiratory infections through cost-effective prevention and behavioral change campaigns.

Key Objectives:
  • Reduce pediatric respiratory infection rates
  • Implement source reduction strategies
  • Conduct behavioral change campaigns
  • Improve child home care management
Adolescent Health

Sexual & Reproductive Health Campaign

Comprehensive campaigns targeting both in-school and out-of-school adolescents with rights-based messaging and interventions focused on sexual and reproductive health rights and education.

Key Objectives:
  • Increase adolescent SRH knowledge and awareness
  • Promote rights-based approach to sexual health
  • Improve access to youth-friendly health services
  • Reduce risky sexual behaviors and outcomes
